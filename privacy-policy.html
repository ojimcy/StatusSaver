1. Introduction

Welcome to StatusVault ("the App"), operated by Rain Protocol Limited ("we", "our", or "us"). This Privacy Policy explains how the StatusVault mobile application (package name: com.statusvault) handles your information when you use our app on Android and iOS devices.

StatusVault allows you to save WhatsApp status updates (images and videos), recover deleted WhatsApp messages (Android only), organize saved content with a favorites system, and supports multiple WhatsApp variants (WhatsApp, WhatsApp Business, etc.).

By installing and using StatusVault, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with any part of this policy, please uninstall the app.

2. Information We Collect

StatusVault is built on an offline-first architecture. We do not collect, transmit, or store any of your personal data on external servers. All information generated through your use of the app remains stored locally on your device.

2.1 Data You Generate Through App Usage

The following data is created and stored locally on your device when you use the app:

Data Type Description Storage Location
Saved Statuses WhatsApp status images and videos you choose to save Device gallery / media storage
Deleted Messages Contact name, message text, group name, timestamp, and thumbnail image (base64-encoded) Local SQLite database
User Preferences Theme settings, auto-delete configuration, and general app preferences AsyncStorage (device)
Temporary Files Cached media files generated during content sharing App cache directory

2.2 Data We Do NOT Collect

  • Personal identifiers (name, email address, phone number)
  • Account credentials or authentication tokens
  • Location or GPS data
  • Device identifiers for our own tracking purposes
  • Usage analytics or behavioral telemetry
  • Contacts or address book data
  • Browsing history or search queries

3. How Your Data Is Stored

All app-generated data is stored exclusively on your physical device. StatusVault does not use cloud storage, remote databases, or any form of server-side synchronization.

  • Saved media (images and videos) are written to your device's gallery or designated media storage folders and are accessible through your default file manager and gallery apps.
  • Deleted message records are maintained in a local SQLite database within the app's private data directory. This database is not encrypted.
  • User preferences are persisted through React Native AsyncStorage, which stores key-value pairs in the app's local data partition.
  • Temporary cache files are held in the app's cache directory and are subject to automatic cleanup by the operating system.

Because data resides solely on your device, it is protected by your device's own security measures (screen lock, encryption, biometrics). If you uninstall the app, all locally stored app data (database, preferences, cache) will be removed by the operating system. Saved media files in your gallery will persist unless you manually delete them.

5. Notification Listener Service

The deleted message recovery feature on Android uses the system's Notification Listener Service. This section provides full transparency on how this sensitive permission operates.

5.1 How It Works

When enabled by you, the notification listener receives callbacks from the Android system each time a notification is posted or removed on your device. StatusVault exclusively processes notifications originating from WhatsApp applications (WhatsApp, WhatsApp Business, and supported variants). Notifications from all other apps are immediately discarded and never read, stored, or processed.

5.2 What Data Is Captured

When a WhatsApp notification is detected and subsequently removed (indicating a deleted message), the following information is extracted and stored locally:

  • Contact or sender name
  • Message text content
  • Group name (if applicable)
  • Timestamp of the notification
  • Notification thumbnail image (stored as base64-encoded data)

5.3 Important Safeguards

  • This feature is opt-in only and requires you to explicitly grant permission in your device's Settings.
  • Non-WhatsApp notifications are never read or stored.
  • All captured data remains in the local SQLite database on your device.
  • No captured message data is transmitted to any external server or third party.
  • You may disable the notification listener at any time through your device's Settings without affecting other app functionality.
Availability Note The notification listener service and deleted message recovery feature are available on Android only. iOS does not provide equivalent system-level notification access to third-party apps.

7. Data Retention & Deletion

Because all data is stored locally on your device, you have full and direct control over its lifecycle.

7.1 Automatic Retention Policies

Data Type Default Retention Notes
Deleted Messages 90 days Automatically purged after the retention period. Duration is configurable by you in app settings.
Buffered Messages 24 hours Temporary message data is cleaned automatically after 24 hours.
Cache Files Managed by OS Temporary sharing files are stored in the cache directory and may be cleared by the operating system as needed.
Saved Media Indefinite Saved images and videos persist in your gallery until you manually delete them.
User Preferences Until app removal Settings persist until the app is uninstalled or data is manually cleared.

7.2 Manual Data Deletion

You can delete your data at any time through the following methods:

  • In-app controls: Use the app's built-in options to clear saved messages, delete individual or all recovered messages, and remove favorites.
  • Clear app data: Navigate to your device's Settings > Apps > StatusVault > Storage > Clear Data to remove all app data including the database and preferences.
  • Uninstall: Removing the app will delete all app-specific data (database, preferences, cache). Saved media in your gallery will remain unless you delete it separately.

8. Data Security

We take reasonable measures to safeguard the data stored by the app, though no method of electronic storage is perfectly secure.

  • Local-only storage: By keeping all data on your device, we eliminate the risks associated with server breaches, network interception, and unauthorized remote access.
  • No network transmission: Your personal data (messages, saved media, preferences) is never transmitted over the internet. The only network activity is loading advertisements through Google AdMob.
  • App sandboxing: On both Android and iOS, the app's private data directory (containing the SQLite database and preferences) is sandboxed by the operating system, preventing other apps from accessing it without root/jailbreak access.
Transparency Notice The local SQLite database used to store recovered deleted messages is not encrypted. While the database is protected by OS-level app sandboxing, users with rooted (Android) or jailbroken (iOS) devices should be aware that other apps with elevated privileges could potentially access this data. We recommend keeping your device's operating system up to date and using your device's built-in security features (screen lock, device encryption).

9. Your Rights Under GDPR

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you are entitled to certain rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and equivalent legislation.

Because StatusVault does not collect or process your personal data on any server, most GDPR rights are inherently satisfied by the app's local-only design:

  • Right of Access: All your data is stored on your device and is directly accessible to you at all times through the app interface.
  • Right to Rectification: You can modify your preferences and manage saved content directly within the app.
  • Right to Erasure: You can delete any or all data at any time using the in-app controls or by clearing the app's data through your device settings.
  • Right to Data Portability: Saved media files are stored in standard formats in your device gallery and can be transferred freely. Recovered messages can be viewed and manually copied.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You can disable specific features (such as the notification listener) at any time through your device settings.
  • Right to Object: You may stop using the app at any time by disabling features or uninstalling.

Regarding advertisements: Google AdMob independently manages GDPR consent for personalized advertising. Users in applicable regions will be presented with a consent dialog managed by Google's User Messaging Platform (UMP) upon first launch or as required.

For any GDPR-related inquiries, please contact us at the address listed in Section 13.
